# INTELLIGENCE BRIEF: The Coca-Cola Company

## Overview

The Coca-Cola Company (NYSE: KO) is the world's largest non-alcoholic beverage corporation, operating in 200+ countries with ~$48B in annual revenue and 65,900 employees. Founded in 1892, it operates a franchise bottling model: selling concentrates/syrups to ~900 independent bottling partners who manufacture and distribute finished products across 500+ products in 200+ brands. The company is executing a multi-year transformation toward a "total beverage company" — diversifying from CSDs into hydration, sports drinks, coffee (Costa), dairy (Fairlife), and selectively entering alcoholic beverages (Topo Chico Hard Seltzer). Market cap currently stands at $337B, making it the 39th most valuable company globally.

## Competitive Landscape

Coca-Cola ranks #3 globally in total beverage sales behind Nestlé ($65.1B) and Anheuser-Busch InBev ($59.8B), but dominates specific categories: #1 in CSDs and bottled water (Dasani = 12% global share). Primary CSD competitors are PepsiCo (~16% U.S. LRB share) and Keurig Dr Pepper ($15.4B sales). The company faces structural threats from fast-growing segments where it's weak: energy drinks (Red Bull, Monster, Celsius), functional sodas (kombucha brands like GT's, Health-Ade), and sports drinks (Gatorade dominates at 61.6% vs. Powerade's ~14%). The $4.82B kombucha market (projected to reach $9.09B by 2033 at 7.6% CAGR) and broader $185B functional sparkling beverage category by 2035 represent the most significant long-term volume risk to traditional CSDs.
