# INTELLIGENCE BRIEF: The Coca-Cola Company

## Overview

The Coca-Cola Company is the world's largest pure-play nonalcoholic beverage corporation, operating in 200+ countries and serving 2.2 billion drinks daily. Founded 1892, headquartered in Atlanta, the company generated $47.94B revenue (FY2025) through an asset-light concentrate/syrup model supported by a global franchise bottler network. It owns 200+ brands spanning sparkling soft drinks, hydration, sports, coffee, tea, juice, and dairy — with 32 billion-dollar brands globally. Publicly traded (NYSE: KO), market cap ~$337B, ranked #39-40 most valuable public company worldwide. The business model generates high margins through concentrate sales (59% of revenue) while bottling partners handle manufacturing and distribution risk.

## Competitive Landscape

Coca-Cola holds #1 global soft drink market share (~40%+ of nonalcoholic beverage sector) but faces intense pressure from PepsiCo ($93.93B revenue, more diversified with food portfolio), Keurig Dr Pepper (most direct CSD competitor with Dr Pepper/7 Up/Snapple portfolio), and specialty players Red Bull/Monster in energy drinks. Structural headwinds include Gen Z consuming 20% less soda than prior generations, rapid growth of functional beverages ($151.8B→$239.95B 2026-2031), and bottled water overtaking CSDs as largest U.S. category by volume. Only two CSD brands grew volume in 2024: Coca-Cola and Sprite — both company-owned, demonstrating brand strength despite category pressure. Stock outperformed PepsiCo by 24 percentage points TTM to March 2025; market cap advantage of $93B over PepsiCo despite half the revenue.
